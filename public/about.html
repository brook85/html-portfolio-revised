About Me

I have always been someone who values knowledge and perseverance, even from my early years. Growing up, I was fascinated by how things worked — I would take apart radios, open up flashlights, and wonder how electricity brought light and sound into our homes. That curiosity stayed with me through school, where I often gravitated toward math and science, even when they were challenging. My family and my faith gave me strength to keep pushing forward, even when resources were limited or the path wasn’t clear. After much dedication, I graduated in electrical engineering, which was a proud milestone in my life. But I didn’t stop there. I realized quickly that a degree was only the beginning, and real understanding comes from continued self-study and application. That’s why I’ve committed myself to lifelong learning — reading books, taking online courses, and exploring new technologies beyond what was taught at the university. This mindset has helped me grow not only as an engineer but also as a person who appreciates the beauty of learning and hard work. My history — filled with struggles, achievements, and self-discovery — shaped me into someone who strives to improve every day, no matter the obstacles.

After graduating in electrical engineering, I came to see that education doesn’t end with a diploma. In fact, that’s when the real learning began for me. I started to study independently, diving deeper into topics that interested me and exploring areas that the classroom never fully covered. This journey of self-study has been deeply rewarding. It has allowed me to develop problem-solving skills, stay updated on new technologies, and expand my understanding beyond the basics. Looking back at my history — from a curious child to a dedicated student and now a self-driven learner — I am proud of how far I’ve come.

My past has shaped me into who I am: a curious, determined, and self-motivated person. Graduating in electrical engineering was just the beginning; I continue to study and grow every day. From my childhood fascination with electricity to my self-studying habits today, I carry forward a passion for learning. My history reminds me that perseverance, faith, and curiosity can light the way toward a meaningful and fulfilling journey.
